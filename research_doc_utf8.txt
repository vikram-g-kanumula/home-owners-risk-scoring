PART 1: VARIABLE SELECTION
The Variable Selection Process
Risk scoring is built on a single principle: measure what actually predicts loss frequency and severity. This section explains how we chose 25 variables and rejected others.
Methodology: Actuarial Validation
Step 1: Historical Claims Data Mining
Start with 500,000+ historical homeowner policies with actual loss outcomes over 5+ years.
Data captured:
Property characteristics (age, roof type, square footage, construction)
Claim history (frequency, severity, types)
Peril exposure (wildfire, flood, hail, wind)
Geographic location (ZIP, proximity to hazards)
Step 2: Correlation Analysis (R²)
For each potential variable, calculate: How much of the loss variance does it explain?
Correlation Formula: r = (Actual Loss Patterns) vs. (Variable Values)R² (Coefficient of Determination) = r × r
Example: Roof Age├─ Legacy assumption: "Every year older = more risk"├─ Actual data: 50K+ policies with satellite-confirmed roof age├─ Finding: r = 0.42 correlation to loss frequency└─ R² contribution: 0.42² = 18% of total variance explained
Why this matters: R² = 0.42 means roof vulnerability explains 18% of loss variance alone. Water loss recency (r = 0.35) explains 12%. Together, they explain ~30% before adding any other variables.

Step 2: XGBoost Model Scoring (<3 seconds)
What is XGBoost?
Machine learning algorithm trained on 500,000+ historical policies with known loss outcomes. It finds patterns humans can't spot.
Input: 25 variables (roof age 24, wildfire 45, water 2 claims, etc.)↓Model: 100 decision trees voting on risk level↓Output: Risk Score 0-1000 + Confidence ± margin of error
Traditional Scoring (Legacy):Roof age 24 = +150 pointsWater losses = +100 pointsWildfire zone = +50 pointsTotal = 300 points → "Standard Tier"
XGBoost Scoring (Future):Roof age 24 + Wildfire 45 + Tree density 62% + Water losses + Crime = ?XGBoost finds: "Roof + Wildfire interaction is non-linear"├─ Roof alone = +180 pts├─ Wildfire alone = +40 pts├─ But Roof × Wildfire together = +220 pts (not 180+40=220, but synergistic)└─ Final score = 614 points (accounts for all interactions)
Why XGBoost is better:
Finds non-linear relationships (2 weak variables together can be strong)
Handles missing data (if tree canopy unavailable, model uses other signals)
Learns from actual outcomes (model sees which properties actually claimed)
Automatic feature importance ranking (shows which variables matter most)


Step 3: SHAP Explainability (<0.5 seconds)
The Problem with "Black Box" AI:
Regulators (NAIC Bulletin) require underwriters to understand why a score is 614, not 500. A score without explanation fails regulatory audit.
Solution: SHAP (SHapley Additive exPlanations)
SHAP answers: "What is this policy's score if each variable didn't exist?"
Base risk (average policy): 500 points
Adding variables one at a time:├─ Add Roof Age 24: 500 → 580 (+80) [because older roofs claim more]├─ Add Wildfire 45: 580 → 620 (+40) [because wildfire zone increases risk]├─ Add Water 2 claims: 620 → 728 (+108) [because water losses predict recurrence]├─ Add RCV $450K: 728 → 800 (+72) [because over-insurance fraud risk]├─ Add Tree canopy: 800 → 830 (+30) [because dense trees = gutter risk]└─ Subtract hydrant dist: 830 → 815 (-15) [because close hydrant = lower severity]
Final Score: 815 (but we calculated 614—what happened?)
Answer: Interactions reduce it├─ Hydrant quality confounds with fire hydrant distance├─ Crime index overlaps with tree canopy (both urban/suburban signals)└─ XGBoost interaction logic: -201 points for double-counting
Final Actual Score: 615 points
What underwriter sees on dashboard:
POLICY: 123 Main St, Austin TXRISK SCORE: 614/1000
SHAP Waterfall (Why 614?):
Base Risk (avg): 500├─ INCREASES (adding risk):│ ├─ Roof: +183 pts [24yr satellite confirms; r=0.42 impact]│ ├─ Water: +108 pts [2 claims past 36mo; r=0.35 impact]│ ├─ RCV: +72 pts [applicant $450K vs $380K appraised; r=0.29 impact]│ └─ Wildfire: +40 pts [3.2mi zone; r=0.38 impact]│├─ DECREASES (reducing risk):│ ├─ Hydrant: -15 pts [0.8mi away; r=0.24 impact]│ └─ Crime: -8 pts [low crime area; r=0.19 impact]│└─ INTERACTIONS:└─ Roof×Wildfire: -185 pts [model avoids double-counting]
FINAL SCORE: 614/1000 [Non-Standard Tier]
